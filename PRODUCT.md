# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Researchers and engineers who construct reproducible smart-home simulations, inspect the
relationship between behavior and sensor observations, and export datasets for activity
recognition or process-mining experiments. They work primarily on desktop displays, need dense
technical context, and must be able to trace every visual conclusion back to a versioned source
artifact.

## Product Purpose

Provide one local, end-to-end workspace for authoring input, deterministic home and sensor
materialization, simulation, ground-truth inspection, replay, and reproducible dataset export.
Success means the full M1-M7 workflow is usable without a CLI or manual JSON editing while
producing exactly the same validated artifacts and semantic digests as the authoritative headless
services.

## Brand Personality

Precise, calm, inspectable. The application should feel like a trustworthy spatial research
instrument: confident enough for expert work, restrained enough to keep attention on the home,
timeline, evidence, and provenance.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS analytics dashboards built from interchangeable statistic cards.
- Decorative smart-home illustrations that hide or simplify authoritative geometry.
- Neon control-room styling, glass panels, ornamental gradients, or gratuitous animation.
- Consumer home-automation apps that obscure IDs, provenance, uncertainty, and failure detail.
- Canvas-only editors that exclude keyboard or structured-data workflows.

## Design Principles

1. Show the authoritative object first: home, timeline, trace, or sensor field owns the surface.
2. Make provenance one interaction away: every conclusion links to its source, version, and digest.
3. Separate observation from truth: observable data and oracle context never blur together.
4. Preserve expert flow: use familiar controls, compact density, durable state, and predictable
   navigation.
5. Fail visibly and recover safely: invalid, incomplete, or corrupt work is never presented as
   publishable scientific output.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. Support complete keyboard navigation, visible focus, screen-reader labels and
live status, reduced motion, non-color status cues, light and dark contrast, adjustable density,
and structured alternatives for every canvas, timeline, chart, and spatial interaction.
